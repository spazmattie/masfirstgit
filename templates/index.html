What is the Effect of Socioeconomic Factors and Race on the Ratio of Men to Women Pursuing STEM Careers Within the NYC Region?



There is a huge gender divide in the area of STEM (Science, Technolody, Engineering, and Mathematics). According to an article linked here, women make up only 28% of the occupations in STEM fields (while men make up 72%), despite them making up approximately 50% of the labor market in general. There are many factors that could contribute to this statistic, including the ethnicity, economic status, and performance in school of an individual, or in this case, an entire group of individuals. We (the creators) looked at the 5 boroughs of New York City, which consists of Manhattan, Brooklyn, The Bronx, Queens, and Staten Island, and related the test scores of public school students influenced by many factors, including ethnicity, gender, and economic status.
