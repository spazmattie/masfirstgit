Regent Exam Scores of Students in New York City

In New York City, students take exams called regent exams to measure student achievement in high school level courses.There are exams for basically all categories of subjects, and all students must pass at least five (1 in English, math, science, social studies, and any other option approved by the state) in order to graduate. We decided to look at all of the STEM subjects (all math and science exams) and compare the scores based on race and gender. The results are below.

How Did They Perform?


As you can see, there is a pretty clear pattern when it comes to race. Whites and Asians are consistently scoring higher than Hispanics and Blacks on average. The only exception is in the subject of Common Core Algebra 2, where Whites scored lower than Hispanics on average in 2018, and lower than both Blacks and Hispanics on average in 2019.

When we compared the genders, we noticed that the gap was way less apparent. There is a very slight gap in the scores between men and women, with the only subjects that had no overlap being Physics (boys scored higher on average) and Common Core Algebra (girls scored higher on average).
